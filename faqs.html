How does the typhoon develop over time?

Typhoons form when strong winds blow into warm areas of the ocean. These winds pick up moisture and rise, bringing colder air with them. This creates pressure, causing the winds to move quickly. The winds revolve or spin around a central point known as an eye.


How do the landforms and bodies of water affect the typhoon?

Typhoons are influenced by land forms and sea bodies based on the amount and type of energy they produce. In general, water and moisture have a significant impact on how the typhoon moves and how much rain, wind, and rotation to expect.
